Definition of Green Infrastructure

Green infrastructure refers to a network of natural and engineered systems that mimic natural processes to manage water sustainably. Examples include wetlands, bioswales, and green roofs, which help retain, filter, and slow down water flow reducing reliance on traditional gray infrastructure like pipes and sewers.


Benefits of Green Infrastructure

  • Flood Reduction: Absorbs excess, rainwater, preventing overflow into urban areas.
  • Improved Water Quality: Filter pollutants before water reaches rivers and oceans.
  • Biodiversity Support: Creates habitats in urban rivers and oceans.
